How to help lower your costs

If you don’t have insurance or have limited insurance, there are a couple of approaches you might consider to help you pay for your medical need.

  1. Evaluate the hospitals in your area for cost effectiveness

    This database will allow you to search for the ‘Sticker Price (the amount a hospital charges for a specific procedure) and the average amount paid by Medicare (the amount Medicare actually writes in a check to the hospital). Hospitals with lower charges will generally be more reasonably priced for you if you are paying on your own.

  2. Negotiate your payment in advance

    Most hospitals will offer you a “Cash Pay” or “Self-pay” price when you schedule an expensive procedure. Usually, that price is a 30% or 40% from their normally charged price. Sounds good, right? Nope! The data in this application shows that the ‘Sticker Price’ is often 5 or even 10 times what Medicare pays. So, when you negotiate, make sure you get a deal that is around or less than the Medicare paid amount.

  3. Get help negotiating your hospital bill--visit Medical Billing Advocates of America:
    • Go to http://www.billadvocates.com/
    • Click on “Find a Medical Bill Advocate” and fill out your information
    • Someone will get back to you with suggestions on advocates in your area
